How these tests reflect ageing

Four systems that decline
as you age — we measure all of them.

As we age, four physiological systems decline in a predictable sequence. The rate of that decline determines how well — and how independently — you age. Each test in MORROW Insights measures one of these systems directly.

01 — VO₂ max
Declines first

Cardiovascular fitness

VO₂ max measures how efficiently your heart delivers oxygen and how well your muscles use it — your cardiovascular engine capacity. It naturally declines with age, and the research literature links lower VO₂ max to reduced cardiovascular reserve and diminished physiological resilience. †

Simple explanation: A stronger engine means a younger biological profile.
02 — DEXA scan
Declines with age

Muscle mass & body composition

Healthy ageing means preserving muscle and limiting the accumulation of visceral fat around organs. DEXA gives a precise picture of both — lean muscle mass, visceral fat, bone density, and left-to-right balance across your body.

6 measurements · Involves low-dose X-ray · Not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding
03 — Strength & power
Power declines faster than strength

Functional reserve

Strength shows how much reserve your body has. Power — the ability to generate force quickly — declines faster and is a key measure of functional capacity. Force plate jump test, isometric mid-thigh pull, and grip strength are widely used assessments in the research literature. ‡

3 assessments
04 — Spirometry
Gradual decline over time

Lung function

Your lungs fuel everything else. Lung capacity gradually declines over time, and reduced lung function is associated with lower stamina and reduced physiological resilience. Spirometry measures how much air you can move and how efficiently your lungs work — combined with ECG and full vitals.

7 measurements including ECG, BP, SpO₂

What exactly am I buying?
A longevity-focused health screening built to improve your healthspan — measuring how much physiological reserve your body has and guiding what to focus on next. In one visit (~2 hrs 40 mins), you complete 40+ tests covering cardiovascular fitness, body composition, strength and power, lung function, blood biomarkers, and psychological well-being. You then attend a 90-minute educational group session where the clinical team explains your results and what they mean for your health priorities. You receive a full results report. S$599. Screening conducted physically in Singapore.
Does the group session replace a doctor consultation?
No. The Educational Group Discussion is a structured educational session — not a medical consultation. It explains your results and the research behind each test. For a dedicated 1:1 review of your results with a doctor, that is available as a separate add-on after the group session.
How is this different from a regular health screening?
MORROW Insights is focused on improving your healthspan — built to guide what to do next, and to track progress over time. It measures physiological reserve across four systems that decline with age (cardiovascular fitness, muscle mass and composition, strength and power, lung function), alongside blood markers and psychological well-being. These tests provide a meaningful baseline for health monitoring, not a one-off snapshot.
Does this replace my GP?
No. MORROW Insights is designed to complement your existing healthcare. The detailed baseline we provide can be shared with your GP or specialist — most of what we measure isn't routinely tested. We flag anything that warrants further investigation.
